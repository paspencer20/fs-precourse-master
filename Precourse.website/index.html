Hi, I'm Patricia.


I'm a new Galvanize student in San Francisco ready to embark on a career as a web developer.

About Me


San Francisco has been my home for nearly twenty years. I was born in Yokosuka, Japan. When I was two, my family and I moved to the Rolla, Missouri so my father could attend university. After he graduated, we moved to Oklahoma City. I consider Oklahoma City my hometown since I lived there from age seven to twenty. A couple of months after my twentieth birthday, I moved to New York City. At first I worked at the Strand Bookstore and shared a tiny studio apartment in the West Village. A couple of years later I was living in Hoboken, working at New York University and taking classes part-time. Eventually, I moved to Highland Park, New Jersey to attend Rutgers University where I majored in history. After graduating from Rutgers, I moved to San Francisco to attend law school at University of San Francisco. I graduated and passed the bar in 2000, but early on I realized I hated being an attorney. So, I started working as a legal assistant/paralegal. Eventually, I ended up working for an environmental law firm and stayed for nearly ten years before taking some time off to figure out something different to do with my life.

Earlier this year, I happened upon coding courses on Codeacademy.com and Codeschool.com. After taking a few, I was hooked and wanted to learn more and realized I wanted to make it a career. Galvanize is the perfect place to pursue my passion for programming and eventually begin a new career.

My Family

My parents, Lon and Keiko, recently divorced. My father continues to live in Oklahoma, and my mother moved to San Francisco in February. My mother and I share a condo in the Richmond. I have a brother, David. He is eight years younger than me. He followed me out to San Francisco about ten years ago. He now lives in Berkeley with his wife Ting.

Hobbies

My favorite hobbies are reading books, travelling to New York City, Europe, and Japan, and volunteering at Muttville (a senior dog rescue organization). I also enjoy spending time with my dog (Daisy), as well as my cats, Neko (the tortoiseshell cat) and Coco (the black cat).

Japan

Japanese language and culture is a big part of my life. I spent several years studying the language and considered a career as a translator. During the past couple of years, my mother and I have travelled to Japan several times. We visited many cities and towns all over central Japan. On our next trip, hopefully next year, we plan to explore the northern regions of Japan around Hokkaido.
